How to cure a hangover in 2026

While there's no quick fix, several home remedies can help alleviate symptoms and support your body's recovery:

  • pHydration and Electrolyte Replenishment:
  • Drink Water: Alcohol causes dehydration, so sipping on water is crucial to rehydrate your body.
  • Electrolyte-rich Beverages: Alcohol can deplete electrolytes, so consider drinking electrolyte beverages like coconut water or broth-based soups to replenish them. Coconut water is rich in potassium, which helps rehydrate the body. Lemon water with a pinch of sugar and salt can also help replenish lost electrolytes.
  • Blood Sugar Support:

    Eat Carbohydrates: Alcohol can lower blood sugar levels, contributing to fatigue and headaches.

    • Bland foods like toast or crackers can help stabilize blood sugar. Honey: Honey contains fructose, which might help your body metabolize alcohol more efficiently.
  • Relief for Nausea and Upset Stomach: Ginger: Ginger can help reduce nausea and vomiting, common hangover symptoms. Consider sipping ginger tea. Eggs: Eggs contain cysteine, an amino acid that may help your body break down acetaldehyde, a toxic byproduct of alcohol metabolism that contributes to hangover symptoms. Peppermint: Peppermint can help relax the intestines and relieve accumulated gas, easing digestive distress. Other Potentially Helpful Remedies:
  • Rest: Getting enough sleep helps your body recover from a hangover and eliminate toxins. Pain Relievers: Over-the-counter pain relievers can help with headache and body aches. However, avoid acetaminophen (Tylenol) as it can be toxic to the liver when combined with alcohol. NSAIDs like ibuprofen may be a better option but can irritate the stomach. B Vitamins and Zinc: Some studies suggest that consuming foods and beverages rich in B vitamins and zinc may result in less severe hangovers. Ginseng: Ginseng may help your body metabolize alcohol faster, potentially reducing hangover symptoms. Milk Thistle: This herb has been used to support liver health and may help relieve indigestion and digestive distress associated with hangovers.

    Things to Avoid: More Alcohol ("Hair of the Dog"): While it may temporarily ease symptoms, it ultimately delays your body's recovery. Greasy Breakfasts: Don't load up on a heavy, greasy meal. Acetaminophen (Tylenol): This medication can be harmful to your liver when combined with alcohol.
